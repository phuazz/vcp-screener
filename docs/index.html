Strategy backtest — indicative only

Read this before the numbers. This backtest runs over the current seed names on yfinance data and is therefore biased upward. Treat it as a sanity check on the rules, never as a forward return estimate. The three ways it is silently flattered:
  1. Survivorship and selection bias (dominant). The universe is today's leaders, included precisely because they already worked. Only point-in-time data removes this.
  2. Look-ahead is avoided, but the universe is not. The base and pivot use only prior data and breakouts fill from their own bar — yet the choice of names is hindsight.
  3. Fills are modelled with slippage (entries and stops gap), but real breakout execution is still harder than any simulation.
The strategy is a risk-controlled sleeve: each trade risks a fixed fraction of equity, capital sits in cash when no setup qualifies, and idle cash earns a conservative money-market yield. Compare it to the Russell 1000 (IWB) on risk-adjusted terms (Sharpe, drawdown), not raw return — its low average exposure caps absolute CAGR by design.

Method & caveats

Screen. Trend Template (price above rising 50/150/200-day averages, within 25% of the 52-week high, RS-rank floor) gates every name. The VCP detector then isolates the base at the highest swing high and measures the pullback sequence with a ZigZag rule, requiring tightening contractions, dry volume on the right, and a tight final contraction.

Backtest rules. Enter on a breakout above the pivot (slippage applied); initial hard stop at the last contraction low; trail on a close below the 50-day average after a short grace period. Position size risks a fixed fraction of equity between entry and stop, capped per name, with a portfolio concurrency limit. Costs: commission plus asymmetric slippage (entries gap more than exits). Idle cash earns a conservative yield.

Data. Prototype source is yfinance — no delisted history, best for liquid large caps. Production should move to a survivorship-bias-free, point-in-time feed (Norgate) before any number here is trusted forward. Method and code: github.com/phuazz/vcp-screener.
